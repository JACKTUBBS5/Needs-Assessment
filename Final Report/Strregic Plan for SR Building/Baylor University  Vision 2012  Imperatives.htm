A Comprehensive List of Imperatives

The following emerges from the preceding vision and will focus our efforts to achieve academic and community excellence by 2012.

Academic resources

- Recruit and retain faculty members who combine strong teaching abilities with a commitment to scholarship and to the Christian mission of Baylor

- Reduce the student-faculty ratio to 13:1 to facilitate development of writing- and speaking-intensive programs and to enhance student classroom experiences

- Increase endowment to support endowed chairs and professorships

- Endow all music programs at levels sufficient to remain competitive in recruiting young artists to Baylor University at both the undergraduate and graduate levels

New Schools and Programs

- Create a Great Texts Program

- Create an Honors College that incorporates the Honors Program, the University Scholars Program, the Baylor Interdisciplinary Core, and the new Great Texts Program

- Create a School of Communication

- Create new doctoral programs in three or more areas from among history, classics, social work, nursing, economics, music, sociology of religion, and political science

- Develop new niche-based Ph.D. programs in Teacher Education, Higher Education and Policy, and in Exercise Physiology, Health Education, or Sport Management

- Develop a Health Sciences Center

Academic Curriculum

- Implement over time a six-hour interdisciplinary Great Texts sequence exploring the basic questions of life and human experience, to be required for all Baylor students

- Expand the number of core courses taken in common by Baylor undergraduates

- Develop the interdisciplinary dimension of courses

- Develop writing- and speaking-intensive courses that prepare our students to communicate with clarity and grace

- Reformulate and strengthen the core curriculum requirements in the fine arts and continue to develop the Theater and Arts programs, the Art and Soul Conference, the Art Lectures sponsored by the Allbritton Institute, and the Beall Poetry Festival

- Revise curricula in the majors to establish prerequisites and sequential learning

- Reform the teacher education curriculum to give greater emphasis to interdisciplinary learning and field-based experiences as preparation for the K-12 school setting

Student Support and Recruiting

- Recruit students who combine strong academic records and possess a character compatible with Baylor's aims

- Enhance significantly the student scholarship general endowment fund to offer scholarships all the way down to our median credentials level

- Strengthen admissions standards consistent with those standards of the Tier One schools in the United States while enriching Baylor's historic commitment to a student profile which includes leadership, service, and character

- Expand freshman seminars to aid students' transition from high school to the University

- Create a Baylor Academic Success Center, located prominently on campus, which will house a program aimed at increasing both student retention and graduation rates by 10% by the year 2012

- Improve the freshman retention rate from 83% to 93% by 2012

Institutes and Centers

- Create a new interdisciplinary graduate research center drawing doctoral students and faculty from graduate programs across the campus to focus on religious phenomena and ethics-based critiques of culture

- Create interdisciplinary graduate research centers for the health sciences, environmental studies, and ethics

- Create a "Center for Higher Education Leadership" that provides graduate and continuing education opportunities for leaders in higher education from across the nation and the world, especially those in Christian universities and colleges

- Augment traditional academic programs with multi- and interdisciplinary centers and institutes to fill distinctive educational niches and encourage innovative research and scholarship

- Encourage cross-disciplinary team-teaching and promote the activities of institutes and centers

Global Education

- Strengthen our existing international programs, develop closer relationships with leading universities throughout the world, and strengthen the teaching and study of foreign languages

- Expand opportunities for international experience for graduate students and faculty

- Develop an international student and scholar alumni network

- Increase the participation of Baylor undergraduate students in a study abroad experience to 30%

Graduate Education and Research

- Increase faculty/student research with both undergraduate and graduate students

- Increase the quantity of widely cited publications to levels associated with faculty at strong graduate programs nationally

- Increase external grants to support graduate students, especially in the sciences, and increase University stipends for graduate students to levels competitive with strong graduate programs nationally

- Heighten doctoral production per program to a minimum of two or three graduates per year

Professional Education

- Develop the School of Nursing infrastructure and resources to support a doctoral program in Nursing

- Continue to build a top theological seminary committed to ministerial education and practice, grounded in a Baptist and evangelical perspective and dedicated to the preparation of church leaders who embrace a worldwide vision

- Reduce our current average law school enrollment to become even more highly selective and to further enhance the learning environment

- Develop, hire, and retain a critical core of business faculty who have a national reputation in their respective disciplines, who are regularly engaged in discussions on issues of national importance such as healthcare and ethics, and who can lead or be involved in a limited number of nationally recognized institutes and centers in the Hankamer School of Business in the areas of emerging technologies, entrepreneurship, global business, professional selling, and nonprofit organizations

Academic Facilities and Technology

- Construct a world-class science building that will host the full complement of natural science teaching, research, and experimentation on campus

- Construct the Harry and Anna Jeanes Discovery Center and all phases of the Sue and Frank Mayborn Natural Science and Cultural History Museum Complex

- Renovate Morrison Constitution Hall

- Improve Engineering and Computer Sciences facilities by providing upgraded space for computing and engineering laboratories

- Augment the fine arts programs through the completion of Phase II of the music building project

- Enhance the library as a physical space that enriches academic community and provides access to the full range of intellectual resources, regardless of format or location through both library collections, as necessary, and especially electronic media

- Renovate and expand the pool facilities at the Baylor marina for use in health, human performance, and recreation academic programming

- Provide necessary financial and human resources to ensure that the University stays abreast of emerging technologies and that those technologies are used effectively and appropriately to enhance the educational experience for students and the teaching and research efforts of faculty

Facilities for Community Life and Infrastructure

- Construct new residence facilities on campus that use the best practices of design to enhance the community and academic experience

- Create green spaces and walking malls along the axes of campus

- Renovate the Student Union Building

- Construct the Stacy Riddle Panhellenic Building

- Build a new intramural fields complex for student recreational activity

- Create a prayer garden

- Renew the facades of selected academic buildings

- Give increased attention to landscaping and works of art

- Build parking garages at the periphery of campus

- Further develop the leisure and academic corridors along both sides of the Brazos River

- Construct a Baylor retirement village

Student Life

- Create a truly residential campus by increasing to at least fifty percent the number of Baylor students in University housing

- Strengthen student life programming to provide opportunities for all classes of Baylor students during all days of the week

- Develop multiple opportunities, both curricular and co-curricular, for students to discern and to understand their life and work as both a stewardship and a calling

- Expand the chapel program to reach graduate students and faculty, reinvigorate small group ministry through Baptist Student Ministries, and institute a range of generation-specific spiritual formation activities

Staff

- Recruit, retain and develop staff members who combine strong professional skills with a commitment to service and the Christian mission of Baylor

- Develop an on-going communication plan to educate staff on major academic programs, higher education issues and student life trends

The Extended Baylor Family

- Direct resources to support life-long learning and relationship-building for our alumni, developing programs that offer alumni opportunities for career networking, degree updating, mentoring current students, and fund raising

- Build contacts with early high school and pre-high school students as part of a program for recruiting larger numbers of students of outstanding intellectual ability and achievement who also evince character, potential for leadership, a heart for service, and compatibility with Baylor's Christian mission

- Develop a broader array of strategies for communicating with alumni, reengage alumni with whom we have lost contact, and collect, maintain, and analyze information from alumni to create rich databases for understanding them, serving them, and soliciting their support

- Cultivate broader and deeper contacts with parents of present and former students, Baptists and other Christians who share our vision for intellectual excellence, churches, residents of Central Texas, and governmental, regulatory, and political leaders with whom we interact

Athletics

- Integrate student-athletes more fully into the life of Baylor University

- Finish in the top five ranking of all sports in the Big Twelve Conference point standings while attaining a top twenty-five position annually in the Sears Directors' Cup standings

- Study the feasibility of bringing the University's track and field, football, and other off-campus athletic facilities on campus in an effort to integrate all athletic programming
